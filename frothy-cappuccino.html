frothy vanilla oat milk cappuccino

17.05.2020

It has been a decidedly cool and rainy June so far and, thanks to the many grey days that require a little bit of extra comfort, this rich, velvety oat milk cappuccino has become a breakfast staple.

All you'll need to froth the oat milk is a cafetière. While I used to manage just fine using one cafetière for both the coffee and milk— through performing a cumbersome dance of plunge, pour, wash, repeat, all while hoping my coffee doesn't get too cold— I would highly recommend investing in a second cafetière if it becomes your preferred method for frothing milk.

Ingredients:

—   150ml brewed filter coffee

  (I used 10g of Columbian strength 4 ground coffee with 180ml of off the boil water, leaving to brew for 7 minutes before plunging)

—   150ml unsweetened oat milk

—   Sugar to taste

  (I used 5g demerara sugar)

—   ½ tsp vanilla extract

Method:

1.   Brew your coffee to taste and pour into a large mug.

2.   Warm your milk, sugar, and vanilla extract in a small saucepan over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar has completely dissolved and the milk is steaming.

3.   Pour the milk into a cafetière and plunge quickly and repeatedly to create a smooth foam. Be careful and do not be overly vigorous as hot milk could shoot out of the pouring spout.

4.   Pour the milk foam into the mug on top of the coffee.

5.   Enjoy! Feel free to experiment with adding flavoured syrups to the milk, or by sprinkling a dash of nutmeg onto the finished milk foam.
